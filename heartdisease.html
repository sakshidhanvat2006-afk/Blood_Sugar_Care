Hypertension Risk Factors

Hypertension (High Blood Pressure) is known as the "Silent Killer" because it often has no symptoms but can lead to heart disease, stroke, kidney failure, and other serious health problems if left untreated.

Major Risk Factors

  • High salt (sodium) intake
  • Obesity or overweight
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Smoking and tobacco use
  • Excessive alcohol consumption
  • Chronic stress

Medical & Biological Risk Factors

  • Family history of hypertension
  • Diabetes
  • High cholesterol
  • Kidney disease
  • Age above 40 years

Lifestyle Risk Factors

  • Unhealthy diet (fast food and fried food)
  • Low intake of fruits and vegetables
  • Poor sleep habits
  • Sedentary lifestyle
  • Excess body weight

Prevention Tips

  1. Reduce salt intake.
  2. Exercise at least 30 minutes every day.
  3. Maintain a healthy weight.
  4. Quit smoking and avoid tobacco.
  5. Limit alcohol consumption.
  6. Manage stress through yoga or meditation.
  7. Check your blood pressure regularly.

Pro Tip: A healthy lifestyle and regular blood pressure check-ups can help prevent hypertension.
