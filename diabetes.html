What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a condition where your blood sugar (glucose) stays too high because your body cannot use it properly.

Glucose is the main source of energy for your body. The hormone insulin helps glucose enter your cells. In diabetes, your body either does not make enough insulin or cannot use it effectively.

What Happens in Diabetes?

  • Type 1: The body produces little or no insulin.
  • Type 2: The body cannot use insulin properly.
  • Result: Blood sugar levels become too high.

Why It Matters

If diabetes is not controlled, it can damage the heart, kidneys, eyes, nerves, and blood vessels.

Normal Blood Sugar Targets

  • Before meals: 80–130 mg/dL
  • 2 hours after meals: Less than 180 mg/dL

Note: Blood sugar targets may vary depending on age and medical condition. Always follow your doctor's advice.

Tips to Manage Diabetes

  1. Monitor your blood sugar regularly.
  2. Eat a healthy and balanced diet.
  3. Exercise for at least 30 minutes daily.
  4. Take medicines as prescribed.
  5. Maintain a healthy body weight.
  6. Visit your doctor regularly.

Pro Tip: A healthy lifestyle and regular check-ups help keep diabetes under control.
