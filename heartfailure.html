Mental Health & Heart Disease

Mental health and heart health are closely connected. Stress, anxiety, and depression can increase the risk of heart disease and slow recovery after heart problems.

How Mental Health Affects the Heart

  • Stress hormones raise blood pressure.
  • Increase heart rate and strain on the heart.
  • Lead to unhealthy habits such as smoking, junk food, and lack of exercise.
  • Poor sleep damages heart health.

Emotional Risk Factors

  • Chronic stress
  • Depression
  • Anxiety disorders
  • Loneliness or social isolation
  • Anger and negative emotions

Effects on Heart Disease Patients

  • Slower recovery after a heart attack.
  • Higher risk of future heart problems.
  • Difficulty following medication or lifestyle advice.

Ways to Protect Both Mind & Heart

  • Exercise regularly.
  • Practice yoga or meditation.
  • Sleep for 7–8 hours daily.
  • Talk to family, friends, or a counselor.
  • Avoid smoking and alcohol.
  • Manage stress effectively.
